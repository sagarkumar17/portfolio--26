The Problem

Jio's home broadband app had a fragmented booking and account management experience. Customers were calling support for tasks that should have been self-serve. The onboarding flow had a 52% completion rate — nearly half of new customers couldn't finish setup without help.

Research Approach

15 user interviews with new Jio Home customers. Session replay analysis of 500+ onboarding sessions. Key finding: customers were getting lost at the address verification step — the third screen — and abandoning.

Key Insight

Users weren't confused by the form — they were anxious about commitment. They wanted to know what came next before they entered their address. The flow needed to show the full journey upfront, not hide it.

Defining the Challenge

HMW: "How might we make new customers feel confident about what they're signing up for at every step of the onboarding?" Redesigned the information architecture to show a persistent progress indicator and next-step preview.

Design Decisions

Progressive onboarding with 3-step summary visible before starting, persistent progress bar, address validation inline (not on a separate screen), and a real-time engineer assignment step that made the process feel human.

What I'd Do Differently

The engineer assignment feature — showing a real person's name and estimated arrival — was the highest-impact single design change. Human signals in digital processes dramatically reduce anxiety. I'd apply this principle much earlier in future projects.
