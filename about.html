Design Philosophy

Three things I believe about design — deeply.

Principle 01

Systems before
screens.

A beautiful screen that breaks in context B or fails under scale is not good design. I start with architecture — how components connect, how decisions propagate — before I design a single pixel.

Principle 02

Evidence over
instinct.

My instincts are a starting point, not a conclusion. Every significant design decision I've made has a research artifact behind it — an interview, a journey map, a usability test — that grounds the rationale.

Principle 03

Constraints are
creative fuel.

Enterprise products are full of constraints — technical debt, legacy systems, competing stakeholder needs. I've learned to treat these as design challenges, not blockers. The best solutions emerge from inside the box.
