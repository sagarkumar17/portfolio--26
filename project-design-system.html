The Problem

Before the design system, Jio's product teams were building the same UI components independently — multiple times, with slight inconsistencies. Design reviews had no common language. Developer handoffs were unpredictable. Every new product started from near-zero.

Research Approach

UI audit across 8 products cataloguing 340+ unique patterns. Developer interviews revealing 40% of sprint time on "rebuilding existing patterns". Analysis of Atlassian Design System, Material, and Fluent for architecture reference.

Key Insight

The biggest waste wasn't duplicated visual work — it was duplicated decision-making. Teams were solving the same design problems in slightly different ways, over and over, with no shared record of the rationale.

Defining the Challenge

HMW: "How might we create a single source of design truth that teams want to use, not just have to use?" The adoption strategy was designed before the first component was built.

Design Decisions

Token-based architecture: design decisions live in tokens (color, spacing, radius, elevation), components consume tokens, patterns compose components. Three theme variants: Consumer Light, Consumer Dark, Enterprise. Full Figma library with usage guidelines and anti-patterns for every component.

What I'd Do Differently

Governance was the hardest part — not design. Defining who can propose changes, how they're reviewed, and how deprecations are communicated required as much thought as the components themselves. Build the governance model on day one.
