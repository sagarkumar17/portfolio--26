Challenges

What makes mentorship hard to design for?

Mentorship is deeply personal — the "right" match depends on industry, seniority, communication style, and even personality. Existing solutions were either too transactional (book a 30-min call) or too informal (find someone on LinkedIn). Neither created lasting, measurable relationships.

Challenge 01
Trust & matching
How do you help users trust a mentor they've never met?
Challenge 02
Progress visibility
Showing growth over time without making it feel clinical or corporate.
Challenge 03
Retention beyond first session
Most mentor relationships die after 1–2 sessions without structure.

Reflection

What I'd Do Differently

I focused heavily on the mentee journey and not enough on mentor motivation. Mentors are the supply-side of a two-sided marketplace — without designing for their experience and incentives first, adoption would likely stall. In a V2, I'd design the mentor onboarding and dashboard before touching the discovery flow.
