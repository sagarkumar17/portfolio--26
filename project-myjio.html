The Problem

MyJio is Jio's primary account management app. Despite 450M+ users, plan discovery and recharge flows were generating high support volumes. Users couldn't find the right plan for their needs, and the recharge flow had too many steps.

Research Approach

Quantitative: funnel analysis showing 3 drop-off spikes in the plan selection flow. Qualitative: 12 user interviews with diverse segments (students, families, seniors). Key finding: plan naming conventions were confusing — users couldn't differentiate between similar plans.

Key Insight

Users didn't want more plan options — they wanted fewer, clearer ones presented based on their actual usage. The current design surfaced 40+ plans with technical specs. Users needed recommendations, not a catalogue.

Defining the Challenge

HMW: "How might we make plan selection feel like a recommendation, not a research task?" Introduced a needs-based plan finder as the primary entry point.

Design Decisions

Needs-based flow: 3 questions (heavy/light user, favourite apps, family/individual) → 2–3 recommended plans. Comparison mode for users who want detail. Simplified recharge flow from 5 steps to 2.

What I'd Do Differently

The biggest lesson: at 450M users, even a 1% improvement is enormous. Design decisions at this scale require more rigorous validation — we ran 3 rounds of A/B testing before finalising the plan recommendation algorithm.
