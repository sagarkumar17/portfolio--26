Act One
Process

Understanding who the PayU Partners platform actually serves — and how they fail today.

The Problem

PayU's partner portal had grown organically over 6 years. Different teams had added features in silos — resulting in 4 separate dashboards, 12 navigation tabs, and zero unified data view.

Partners were spending 30+ minutes on tasks that should take 3. Support ticket volume was 4× industry average. The platform's complexity was actively driving merchant churn to competitors like Razorpay.

04
Act Four
Design

From insights to interfaces — a unified platform that serves finance, sales, and ops without compromise.

P.01
Transparency First
Every status should show a reason. Every held payout has an explanation. Every failed action has a next step. Users should never need to guess or call support.
P.02
Role-Aware Defaults
Finance users land on a settlement summary. Sales users land on portfolio health. The same data, surfaced differently based on what each user actually needs first.
P.03
Progressive Disclosure
Show the 3 most important numbers prominently. Surface full detail on demand. Never bury critical info, but never overwhelm casual reviewers with 40 metrics by default.

What I'd Do Differently

I'd invest earlier in a Partner Advisory Board — a small group of power users who join bi-weekly design reviews. The insights we got from 22 contextual interviews were invaluable, but those partners became disconnected from the process once we moved into design. Continuous input would have caught the edge cases we found only during QA.

The mobile-first mandate came late — mid-way through design. I had to retrofit mobile layouts onto desktop-first decisions. Had I established responsive constraints from day one, the mobile experience would have been more cohesive rather than adapted. Especially for Sales persona users who live on mobile.
